Creating a FOIA Request

Drafting Time: Variable
Awesome rating: 40% awesome

FOIA requests are a valuable tools at the disposal of journalists, and every journalist should invest the time to learn how to make one. Below, we'll explore key steps and advice on how to properly craft a FOIA request.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Time
  • Precise Writing Skills
  • Documents for Personal Identification (Optional)
  • Sufficient Monetary Resources (Optional)

  1. PREPARE

    Utilize publicly available information to answer your questions and complete your story as much as possible; and identify what (if any) necessary information is absent from the public domain. If you suspect that the government holds important information which you cannot access through regular channels of research, identify which specific government agency is likely to have it and proceed to write a FOIA request as either an email or letter to them.

  2. BEWARE

    While much information is subject to release at the behest of a FOIA request, the government is protected from releasing some types of information including classified materials.

    Protected information can be redacted in returned FOIA requests.

  3. DRAFT: IDENTIFY YOURSELF

    When you begin drafting your FOIA request, identify your name and give the agency you're writing the contact details they need to respond. Such details include your street address, email address, and phone number. Depending on what information you are requesting, you may need to include legal documentation affirming your identity to the agency.

  4. DRAFT: SPECIFY YOUR TARGET

    Precisely identify what information you want the agency to release to you and what medium you would like to receive it in. Be sure to narrow your search as much as possible so that they return only what you need and no more. Forcing an agency to gather too much information in order to satisfy a broad or vague request may incur costs (see more about fees and fee exemptions in the video below) and leave you with much information to filter through yourself. Furthermore, you may note if you would prefer the agency to provide you with the information you are requesting in a specific format.

  5. SEND AND WAIT

    Once you finish drafting your FOIA request, send it and wait. Acknowledging certain exceptions, the government usually has only twenty working days in which to respond. The agency will confirm that they received your request and assign it a tracking number for you to use to keep tabs on your request's progress online.
